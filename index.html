About

Educator.
Designer.
Strategist.

I'm a bilingual (French/English) learning professional with over a decade of experience designing, facilitating, and evaluating learner-centred programs across corporate and public-sector environments. I began my career delivering business English training to professionals at a Korean telecommunications firm, then spent several years in a unionized public-sector setting deepening my instructional design and adult learning practice.

My background taught me that the best learning is not about being content heavy, but designing the conditions for performance. I bring that same philosophy to every project. Start with the business problem, identify the performance gap, and work backwards to the right solution. I practise ADDIE and Action Mapping, work fluently in Articulate 360 (Storyline and Rise), and have a solid track record navigating complex stakeholder environments.

I communicate fluently in English and French, with working knowledge of Korean and Portuguese. Accessibility is built into my design process from the start, not added at the end.

How I Work

Design that starts
with performance

01
Start with the gap

Every project begins with the business problem, not the content. I use Action Mapping to identify the performance gap before anything goes near a slide.

02
Design for decisions

Adults learn by doing. I favour scenarios, branching, and practice over information delivery. The goal is behaviour change, not content completion.

03
Respect the learner

No padding. No unnecessary clicks. Every element earns its place. I design courses I'd want to take, ones that respect the learner's time and intelligence.

04
Build for access

Accessibility isn't a checkbox. AODA compliance, proper alt text, logical focus order, and inclusive design are built in from the start, not added at the end.
